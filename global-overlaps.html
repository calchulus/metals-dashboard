Does It Matter? Yes — Here's Why

Different overlaps serve different functions depending on the metal:

  • London-NY (10:00-15:00 UTC): Dominant for gold/silver. This is where benchmark prices (LBMA Fix, COMEX settlement) are determined. Spread is tightest here.
  • Shanghai-London (08:00-15:30 UTC): Critical for base metals (Cu, Al, Zn). SHFE closes set Asian direction; LME continues. Gap risk highest at SHFE close (07:30 UTC).
  • Singapore-London (08:00-16:00 UTC): Important for nickel and iron ore. SGX contracts reference LME pricing.
  • Mumbai-London (08:00-16:30 UTC): Relevant for gold in INR terms. MCX prices track LBMA but with FX lag.
  • New York-Shanghai (06:00-09:00 UTC): Brief but critical for overnight copper/steel. USPM data released here moves SHFE opening.

Key takeaway: For precious metals, London-NY is everything. For base metals, Shanghai-London matters more. For industrial metals with thin liquidity, any overlap helps.
