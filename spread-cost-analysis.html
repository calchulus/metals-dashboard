Spread Cost vs Hedge Cost by Holding Period

Key Insight

For sub-1-month trades, spread dominates total cost. Hedging only becomes cost-effective when holding period exceeds the break-even point where annualized hedge cost exceeds cumulative spread+slippage.

  • Intraday trades: Spread is 100% of cost — optimize execution timing
  • 1-week holds: Spread still dominates — avoid thin hours
  • 1-month holds: Hedge cost begins to matter — compare FX exposure
  • 3+ months: Hedge cost may exceed spread — hedge if currency exposed
