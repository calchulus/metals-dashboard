Sheet-Based Rarity: Vintage cards printed on 121-card (11×11) sheets. Cards appearing 8 times (R8) vs 7 times (R7) creates natural rarity variance within the same nominal tier.
Artificial Rarity First: Neo Genesis starter final evolutions (Feraligatr, Meganium, Typhlosion) were deliberately made 2× rarer (~1/82.5 packs) — the first instance of intentional rarity manipulation for gameplay value.
Modern Complexity: SV era has 15+ rarity tiers vs WOTC's 4–5. More tiers = more chase cards = higher box opening rates = more product sold.
121-Card Constant: Commons sheets have been 121 cards (11×11) for 25+ years. Collation principles haven't changed since Base Set.
Sequence Length: Gen 1–5 used 216-pack sequences (1 case). Gen 6+ switched to 360 packs (10 boxes). This affects how duplicates appear in boxes.
Set Completion Math: A 200-card set needs ~1,200 packs (33 boxes) for 90% completion via packs only. Trading/buying singles is always more efficient.
Guaranteed Rare Slot: Modern packs guarantee 1 rare per pack. Reverse holo slot adds ~12.5% chance of a bonus rare. This is why modern box variance is lower than vintage.
Striped vs Sequential Collation: Some Belgian-printed sets (Jungle, Neo Discovery) used striped collation, creating different card sequences and potentially different rarity distributions.
Case Economics: Opening 1 case (216 packs) guarantees at least 1 Special Illustration Rare and gives 92.6% chance of a Hyper Rare. The "guaranteed hit" threshold.
Price vs Rarity Disconnect: PSA population data shows grading demand doesn't always match pull rates. Popular Pokemon (Charizard, Pikachu) command premiums regardless of actual rarity.
